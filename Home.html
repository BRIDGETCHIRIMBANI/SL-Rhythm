Year 1 - Finding your Tribe

You have just arrived on campus and a lot of people are pursuing a degree, just like you. You find something interesting and you are curious about them. You spoke with multiple people during the different orientation sessions and you think you might be vibing with them. You might be asking, "are these the people to whom I belong?", "I wonder if they feel the same?".

  • Team and peer work

HOW DO YOU SUCCEED?

Image Placeholder

  • Skills level awareness with the different leadership core modules
  • Don't put unrealistic expectations on yourself. You are not expected to be an expert, you are expected to develop an appreciation for the practical skills in your modules.
  • Actively unlearn misconceptions you hold about the skills.
  • Engage with the coursework and meeting the practical and non-practical course requirements.

INSTITUTIONAL INVESTMENT

Image Placeholder

  • Engage the Student Representative Counsil (SRC) for Clubs and Societies Fairs at the beginning of the year. In the absence of these, reach out to Student Life to know the process of opening a club/society and the benefits to the community.
  • Apply to work-study opportunities at ALCHE to learn new skills

Internship Cycle

Image Placeholder

  • Be aware of the value and offerings of the Student Professional Development (SPD) team.
  • Be willing to build self-awareness about your strengths, skills, areas of development (things that may not come naturally) and needs as it relates to the world of work.
  • Build a career plan. It is never set in stone; it can change as your journey at ALCHE progresses.
  • Prioritise re-articulating your mission and taking steps to actualise it, in light of the skills that you have gained in Leadership Core and internships.
  • Be willing to build character, humility, the right attitudes, and the mindset needed to succeed in the world of work.
  • Attitude and mindset are more valued than high performance at this stage in your career.
  • Attend the Student Professional Development (SPD) Career Day or coaching sessions dedicated to core issues surrounding internships, e.g., How To Carry Yourself at Work, Managing Expectations, Handling Toxic Work Environments, Building Meaningful Relationships, etc.

Year 2 - Marking your Tribe

MAY BE NEW TO YOU

Image Placeholder

  • Complexity of material
  • The administrative work involved in building a business
  • Discovering and exploring your mission in light of and aligning it to your professional experience
  • Learning Management Systems
  • The practical ramifications of being an entrepreneur, divesting the realities from their fantasies of successful entrepreneurs
  • Prioritizing career learning alongside academic learning, humbly

HOW DO YOU SUCCEED?

Image Placeholder

  • Be 100% sure on the instructions for your assessments.
  • Always create a plan as soon as you have your instructions; it reduces the feeling of being overwhelmed.
  • Impactful communication; mastering the skills needed to pitch your skills virtually and in-person. You must be able to communicate your idea and get people fired up to invest.
  • Engage with academic and wellness support structures within the institution. They help with giving order to the many things that might overwhelm you.
  • Team building; you might get interest after successfully communicating your plan. What are you offering your team, and what are you getting out of your team?
  • Strengthen your collaborative peer relationships.
  • Design thinking and market research creating ventures that support needs.
  • Adopt a work-life balance, ensuring space for rest, exercise, and downtime. It helps you to build structures to avoid burnout.
  • Develop strong personal boundaries around technology, social media, and inter-relationships.

INSTITUTIONAL INVESTMENT

Image Placeholder

  • Keep the goal in mind and remain focused on your ultimate mission.
  • How do your dissertation goals align with the mission you have articulated at the beginning of your student journey?
  • Leap, Writing Centre, and Wellness collaborations for academic writing, ethics, and integrity. Res Life could also start a new program, such as community study marathons where everyone sits in the LC to study for a few hours a day with a coffee break or snacks (especially around exam time).

Year 3 - Leading your Tribe

MAY BE NEW TO YOU

Image Placeholder

  • Consolidating your academic skills for postgraduate applications
  • Consolidating the value you bring knowledge of from your experience and skills. This is best done by distilling 360-degree feedback. Essentially, it is building credibility as a young professional, which is not new in itself, but an evolution of existing knowledge
  • Consolidating your academic skills for honours and capstone projects. Solving the imminent disconnect between your academic and technical skills needed to deliver a convincing honours project. There may be gaps in your theoretical knowledge and the skills you actually need

HOW DO YOU SUCCEED?

Image Placeholder

  • At this point, you must be able to justify your stand on issues, especially using the skills you have learned (appraise, argue, defend, judge, support).
  • Produce new and original work (design, assemble, construct, develop, formulate solutions).
  • Learn technical skills outside of your curriculum; this will set you apart in your honours projects, for instance.
  • Prioritise getting professional certifications to set your applications apart.
